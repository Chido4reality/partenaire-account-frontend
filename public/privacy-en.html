Privacy Policy

Mon Partenaire Dozie · Effective date: 26 May 2026

Mon Partenaire Dozie ("we", "our", or "the app") is a point-of-sale and inventory-management application for small and medium businesses in Cameroon. This policy explains what data we collect, why we collect it, how we store and protect it, and the rights you have over it.

1. Who we are

The data controller for Mon Partenaire Dozie is the operator named in the Contact section below. The app is distributed through the Google Play Store. Source code lives in private repositories; the production service is operated from Cameroon by the contact below.

2. Data we collect

2.1 Account & identity

  • Full name of the account owner and staff members
  • Phone number (used as the login identifier)
  • PIN or password (stored only as a one-way bcrypt hash; we never see the plain text)
  • Role within the business (owner, manager, cashier, warehouse)
  • Optional: profile photo, language preference (English / French)

2.2 Business data you enter

  • Shop name, address, and locations
  • Product catalogue: names, barcodes, prices, costs, stock levels
  • Customer records: names, phone numbers, outstanding balances
  • Sale records, returns, refunds, exchanges
  • Cash shifts, expenditures, transfers
  • Audit log of sensitive actions (who edited what, when)

2.3 Device & technical data

  • Device type, operating system version, app version
  • IP address and approximate location (city-level) at sign-in, for security monitoring
  • Crash reports and error logs (no personal data included)

2.4 What we do not collect

  • We do not access your contacts, photos, microphone, or SMS
  • We do not request precise GPS location
  • We do not use third-party advertising or analytics SDKs that profile users

3. How we use your data

We use the data above only to:

  • Operate the POS and inventory features you signed up for
  • Authenticate users and protect accounts from unauthorised access
  • Generate sales reports, daily summaries, and the receipts your customers expect
  • Process mobile-money payments via our payment partner (see Section 5)
  • Provide customer support when you contact us
  • Fix bugs and improve the service (in aggregate, never targeted advertising)

We do not sell your data. We do not use it for advertising. We do not build behavioural profiles for sale.

4. Legal basis for processing

  • Contract: processing is necessary to provide the service you signed up for.
  • Legal obligation: tax-relevant sale records may need to be retained to comply with Cameroonian commercial law.
  • Legitimate interest: security monitoring and fraud prevention.
  • Consent: for any optional feature that explicitly asks for it (e.g. WhatsApp notifications).

5. Sharing with third parties

We share data only with the service providers we need to operate the app:

  • Supabase — database and authentication hosting
  • Render — application backend hosting
  • Vercel — web frontend hosting
  • CamPay — mobile-money payment processing (when you enable the mobile-money payment method)
  • WhatsApp / Twilio — only if you explicitly enable WhatsApp notifications, and only for the contact phone number you authorise
  • Google Play — app distribution; we never share business data with Google

Each provider is contractually bound to use the data only to deliver their service to us. We never sell or rent personal data to advertisers, marketers, or data brokers.

6. Where your data is stored

Production data is stored in managed Postgres instances operated by Supabase, with backups handled by the provider. Backend servers run on Render. Some processing may occur outside Cameroon (United States or European Union data centres) as part of normal operation of those providers. We choose providers with industry-standard security and encryption-at-rest.

7. How long we keep your data

  • Active account data is kept for as long as your account is open.
  • Sale and tax-relevant records are kept for at least the period required by Cameroonian commercial law (typically up to 10 years).
  • If you close your account, we will delete or anonymise data that we are not legally required to retain, within 90 days of your request.
  • Audit logs are retained for security forensics for up to 2 years.

8. Your rights

You have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Delete your data (subject to the legal retention periods above)
  • Export your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Withdraw any consent you previously gave
  • Object to processing based on legitimate interest
  • Lodge a complaint with the competent supervisory authority in Cameroon

To exercise any of these rights, use the Contact section below. We respond within 30 days.

9. Security

We protect your data with:

  • HTTPS / TLS encryption for all network traffic
  • Bcrypt password and PIN hashing (we never store plain text)
  • JWT-based session authentication with explicit expiry
  • Role-based access control (owner / manager / cashier / warehouse)
  • Owner-PIN approval gate on sensitive actions (void sales, edit credit limits, manual stock adjustments, etc.)
  • Database row-level security policies
  • Encryption at rest, managed by our hosting providers

No internet service can be 100% secure. If we become aware of a breach that affects your personal data, we will notify you without undue delay.

10. Children

Mon Partenaire Dozie is a business tool. It is not intended for and not directed at children under 16. We do not knowingly collect data from children.

11. Cookies and local storage

The web version of the app uses browser local storage to keep your session token and language preference between visits. The Android app uses equivalent on-device storage. We do not use third-party advertising cookies or tracking pixels.

12. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The effective date at the top reflects the latest version. Material changes will be announced in-app before they take effect. Continued use of the app after the effective date constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
